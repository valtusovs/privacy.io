Welcome to X-Privacy.

We created X-Privacy in 2018 with the goal of raising awareness of the issues of the massive collection of personal information online. The leaders on the scoreboard of best tracking and data mining are Google, Facebook and Microsoft.
There are two main reasons why we created this committee. Firstly because we heard the following arguments far too often:
  • "So what, there's nothing I can change about it"
  • "I don't have anything to hide"
Especially the latter argument is a very dangerous attitude, as detailed in the section Importance of Privacy.
Secondly, because we want to make a difference on campus. We don't want to blindly accept that the parts of the administration and professors uses Google Forms, nor that all communication has and therefore always should be on Facebook if there are better alternatives.
Many people associate big data and data mining with technological advancement. They hence draw the conclusion that people who value privacy are against the development of the internet. Just to clarify this: we believe the internet offers great opportunities and should be used. We are not against social media just because we don't love Facebook. These are all not contradictions. We are very lucky to grow up in a society where we have these internet technologies available. The only question we ask ourselves is whether some of the practices of companies like Google and Facebook are really necessary to provide you the services. To show you that it works without collecting unnecessary information from you, we want to inform you about alternative internet services you can use instead without giving up much of the convenience.
